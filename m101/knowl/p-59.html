One way we can simplify expressions is to combine like terms. Like terms are terms where the variables match exactly (exponents included). Examples of like terms would be \(3xy\) and \(-7xy\) or \(3a^2b\) and \(8a^2b\) or \(-3\) and \(5\text{.}\) To combine like terms add (or subtract) the numbers in front of the variables, then keep the variables the same.
