\begin{align*} \text{ \(6.022\times 10^{23}\) atoms yields \(12\) g} \amp\amp\amp \text{Understand what information we are given}\\ \text{Let \(x=\) the mass of one atom of carbon} \amp\amp\amp \text{Convert the given information into an equation}\\ (6.022\times 10^{23})x =12 \amp\amp\amp \text{The number of atoms times the mass of each } \\ \text{ } \amp\amp\amp \text{equals the total mass of \(12\) grams} \end{align*}

Now we solve the equation.

\begin{align*} (6.022\times 10^{23})x =12 \amp\amp\amp \text{Solve for \(x\)}\\ x=\dfrac{12}{6.022\times 10^{23}} \amp\amp\amp \text{Divide both sides by } 6.022\times 10^{23}\\ \dfrac{12}{6.022}=1.9927... \amp\amp\amp \text{Divide the numbers}\\ 1.9927=1.9927\times 10^{0} \amp\amp\amp \text{Convert this number into scientific notation}\\ \dfrac{10^{0}}{10^{23}}=10^{-23} \amp\amp\amp \text{Use quotient rule, using \(10^{0}\) from the conversion}\\ x=1.9927 \times 10^{-23} \amp\amp\amp \text{Our Solution:} \end{align*}

The the mass of one atom of carbon is \(1.9927 \times 10^{-23}\) gram.\(\checkmark\)
