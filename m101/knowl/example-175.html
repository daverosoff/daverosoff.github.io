Example3.B.6Determine Slope Given Two Points

Determine the slope and describe the line.

Determine the slope between \((-4,-1)\) and \((-4,-5)\text{.}\) First identify \(x_1, y_1, x_2, y_2\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} (x_1, y_1)\amp =(-4,-1)\text{ and}\\ (x_2,y_2)\amp =(-4,-5) \amp\amp\text{Use slope formula \(m=\dfrac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}\)}\\ m\amp =\dfrac{-5-(-1)}{-4-(-4)}\amp\amp \text{Simplify}\\ m\amp =\dfrac{-4}{0} \amp\amp \text{Can't divide by zero, undefined}\\ m\amp =\text{does not exist (no slope),} \amp\amp \text{Our Solution}\checkmark\\ \text{vertical}\amp\amp\amp \end{align*}
