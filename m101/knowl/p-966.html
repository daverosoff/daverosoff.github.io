Probability Properties

  1. \(0\leq P(event)\leq 1\text{.}\)
  2. If the \(P(event)=1\text{,}\) then it will happen and is called a certain event.
  3. If the \(P(event)=0\text{,}\) then it cannot happen and is called an impossible event.
  4. The probabilities of all the outcomes in a sample space add up to \(1\text{.}\)
